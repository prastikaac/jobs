Restaurant Waiter in Helsinki


You’ve landed in the perfect spot if you’re seeking for a Restaurant Waiter Job in Helsinki

JK Kitchen Oy is hiring a motivated Cashier-Waiter for our staff restaurant Ravintola Vaunu located in Pasila, Helsinki. Every day we serve 180–300 lunch customers and also provide catering services for meetings within the building.

This is a permanent, full-time role ideal for those with a background in hospitality, students with training in the restaurant field, or anyone passionate about customer service. You’ll enjoy stable working hours, a collaborative team, and the chance to make a real impact in everyday service.

JOB INFORMATION

  • Job Type Full-time, Permanent Position
  • Location Pasila, Helsinki, Uusimaa, Finland
  • Work Hours Mon–Fri, 07:00–16:00 (112.5h / 3 weeks)
  • Experience Previous experience in cashier and customer service roles required
  • Language Finnish (working language)
  • Salary According to agreement (based on skills & experience)

Job Responsibilities

As a Cashier-Waiter in our staff restaurant, you will play a key role in ensuring smooth and friendly service. Your main tasks include:

  • Operating the cash register and handling customer payments
  • Providing excellent customer service and maintaining a positive dining experience
  • Maintaining the dining area and service lines
  • Receiving and managing meeting catering orders
  • Supporting colleagues and ensuring smooth daily operations

Requirements

  • Basic education in the restaurant field
  • Previous experience in cashier and customer service work
  • Valid Hygiene Passport
  • Good interaction and teamwork skills
  • Kitchen experience is considered an advantage

Why Work With Us?

  • Stable, long-term employment in a respected Helsinki restaurant
  • Regular working hours (no late-night shifts)
  • Opportunities to grow and take on more responsibility
  • Friendly and professional work environment

Tips to Help You Get This Job

Increase your chances of success with these tips:

  • Update your CV and highlight any customer service or cashier experience
  • Mention your Hygiene Passport clearly in your application
  • Show flexibility and a positive attitude towards teamwork
  • Be professional, punctual, and customer-oriented
  • If you have kitchen skills, highlight them—they are an advantage

How to Apply?

If this role fits your skills and ambitions, please send your application including your education, work experience, and salary expectation to:

Email: jari.kaunisto@jk-kitchen.fi

For further details, contact Restaurant Owner Jari Kaunisto at 045 673 5324 (available Mon–Fri, 15:00–17:00).

Application Deadline: 23.5.2026, 23:59

How to get Succeed in Getting a Job in Finland ?

For many people moving to a new country, the biggest challenge is understanding how to get a job in Finland. If you are new to the Finnish job market or don’t speak the local language fluently, it may feel overwhelming. However, with the right information, preparation, and resources, you can find open jobs in Finland that match your background. From skilled jobs to part-time or unskilled positions, there are many ways to start your career here.

Begin by updating your CV to follow the European (EU) format. Finnish employers appreciate short, clear resumes that highlight your most recent experience, education, and relevant skills. If you are applying for cleaning jobs in Finland, warehouse jobs in Finland, delivery jobs in Finland, or food packaging jobs in Finland, make sure to mention physical strength, flexibility, and past similar experience—even if it was in another country. For roles like IT jobs in Finland, nursing jobs in Finland, or developer jobs in Finland, make sure your certifications are valid or can be recognized locally.

Knowing where to find jobs in Finland is essential. You can search on websites like findjobsinfinland.fi that collect listings from various sectors, companies, and official job portals. We help you filter and access available jobs in Finland without the confusion of searching across multiple sites. Whether you are looking for remote jobs in Finland, jobs in Finland in English, or city-specific roles such as jobs in Helsinki Finland or jobs in Espoo Finland, centralized platforms like ours are a good place to start.

Language is another important factor. While many worry they can't find a job in Finland because they only speak English, the reality is that many employers are open to hiring English speakers. There are jobs in Finland for English speakers with visa sponsorship in sectors such as IT, logistics, cleaning, caregiving, and hospitality. Companies looking for workers often advertise jobs in Finland for foreigners, and these roles are clearly marked with language requirements and visa support options.

If you are still wondering where to apply jobs in Finland or how easy is it to find a job in Finland, it’s important to remain realistic and flexible. Seasonal jobs such as berry picking jobs in Finland, summer jobs in Finland 2025, and agricultural work are often open to first-time applicants and do not require high qualifications. These roles can provide your first Finnish job experience, after which it becomes easier to apply for more permanent positions.

When applying, be sure to:

  • Customize your application for each job and include a short motivation letter
  • Mention your availability clearly and be honest about your visa or work permit status
  • Include any work experience from your home country, especially if it relates to healthcare, customer service, hospitality, logistics, or maintenance
  • Be prepared to attend interviews in English or Finnish—practicing a few basic Finnish phrases can make a very good impression
  • Apply early, as many jobs in Finland fill quickly, especially part-time or low-skill roles

Finding a job may take time. You might wonder why you still can't find job in Finland, even after applying to many places. The key is to be patient, follow up when possible, and improve your application materials. Also, make use of government-supported employment services and job fairs in Finland that are open to foreigners and students. These events can introduce you to employers hiring immediately and offer real networking opportunities.

Whether you’re aiming for nursing jobs in Finland for international nurses, driver jobs in Finland with visa sponsorship, or even freelance and online jobs in Finland, understanding the local system, writing a good application, and staying motivated will increase your chances of success.

The job market in Finland is structured, well-regulated, and offers a wide range of employment opportunities across many sectors. There are thousands of open jobs in Finland throughout the year, both in urban areas like Helsinki, Espoo, Tampere, and in rural and seasonal regions such as Lapland, Levi, and Joensuu. Whether you are seeking jobs in Finland for students, international professionals, or foreigners with little experience, there are available jobs in Finland suited for you.

Common job sectors include IT jobs in Finland, healthcare jobs in Finland, warehouse jobs in Finland, cleaning jobs in Finland, and logistics and delivery jobs in Finland. In recent years, the demand for roles such as driver jobs in Finland, hotel jobs in Finland, restaurant and food packaging jobs in Finland, and even jobs in Finland with visa sponsorship has steadily increased. This makes Finland an appealing destination for those coming from countries like Nepal, India, Pakistan, the Philippines, Nigeria, Kenya, Bangladesh, and beyond.

Many people wonder how to find a job in Finland or whether getting a job in Finland is hard. The answer depends on your skill set, flexibility, language skills, and willingness to adapt. Whether you’re wondering what are jobs in Finland, what jobs are in demand in Finland, or which jobs are most needed in Finland, the country has something to offer across a wide range of industries. For example, caregiver jobs in Finland with visa sponsorship are currently in high demand due to the aging population. Additionally, there are growing needs for skilled workers in areas like cybersecurity, bioinformatics, software QA, construction, engineering, and teaching. Seasonal work such as berry picking and summer jobs in Finland 2025 also attract a large number of temporary foreign workers each year.

Finland also offers a stable work environment with collective agreements that ensure fair pay and proper working conditions. Many people are curious if jobs in Finland pay well or if salaries are low. While salary levels vary by industry, the cost of living is balanced by strong social systems and employee protections. It’s important to understand that even unskilled jobs in Finland for foreigners often come with legal contracts, paid holidays, and rights not always found in other countries.
